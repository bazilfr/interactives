About Me!

Hello! My name is Bazil Frueh and I am student at Northwestern University studying journalism,
art history, and Integrated Marketing Communications. Here in my portfolio, you'll find various
journalism/media-based projects I've worked on, including audio packages for Northwestern's radio
station WNUR and data visualizatons and maps made in my interactive news class.

Throughout my life, I have always been a storyteller and empathetic listener. Beginning mostly
in creative efforts, my interest in journalism blossomed from my experiences writing poetry and
plays, two hobbies I continue to pursue to this day. Through my time at Northwestern, however, I have fallen
in love with the on-the-go pace of news reporting, leading me to eventually take on roles at
WNUR News and at our campus's first queer-interest publication: CRUSH Magazine. I have gained
a particular interest in popular culture and celebrity news, both of which have served as topics
of interest in my journalistic work.

As I exit my academic journey and enter the career world, I am eagar to expand my curiousity
and flex the skills that I've gained over the last few years. I hope to continue making audio
stories by gaining an internship at one of NPR's local affiliates, where I'd be able to gain
hands on experience through beat reporting and in-depth interviewing.

Thanks for taking the time to look through my portfolio. I'd be happy to connect and chat!
